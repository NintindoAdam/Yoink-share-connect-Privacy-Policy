Yoink Privacy Policy

Last updated: 6 July 2026

Yoink does not collect your data. Everything you create lives on your device and is only shared when you choose to share it, directly with the person in front of you.

What Yoink stores

Yoink is a digital business card app. The card you build, the contacts you receive, and your app settings are stored only on your device, using your iPhone's local storage. None of it is sent to us, because there is no server to send it to.

What we collect

Nothing. Yoink has no analytics, no tracking, no advertising, and makes no network requests to any server we operate. We do not have accounts on our end, we do not receive your card, and we cannot see how you use the app.

Sign in with Apple

Yoink uses Sign in with Apple to create a local account identifier on your device. This identifier is stored on your device only and is never transmitted to us or anyone else. We do not run a server, so there is no copy of your Apple credential anywhere outside your phone and Apple's own systems.

Sharing a card

When you share your card, it travels directly between the two devices — by scanning a QR code or a nearby peer-to-peer connection. The data goes from your phone to theirs. It does not pass through us.

Opening links

Your card can include links to your social media or website. When you or someone else taps one of those links, iOS opens it in the relevant app or browser. That action is handled by Apple and the destination service, under their own privacy terms, not ours.

Deleting your data

You are always in control. From the app's settings you can:

  • Delete your account, which removes your local account identifier.
  • Delete your card and any contacts you've saved.

Because everything is stored on your device, deleting the app also removes all of your Yoink data. There is nothing left on a server for us to hold, because there is no server.

Children

Yoink is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, regardless of age.

Changes to this policy

If Yoink ever adds a feature that changes how data is handled — for example, optional cloud sync — this policy will be updated before that feature ships, and the date at the top will change.
